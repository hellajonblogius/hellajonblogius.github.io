the morning after my encounter with the halfworm, the one who confirmed paul's sad fate, i had considered surrendering myself to the earth. i contemplated squirming to the top of a mound and waiting for a bird to snatch me away from the wretched earth.

as i meddled over these foolish thoughts, rolling, dark clouds formed in the sky. water began to fall from the sky once again. the dirt below me moistened, releasing the familiar fragrance that stirred my soul into motion once before. it reminded me of the first night on the pavement. the fear, the excitement. i remembered the following afternoon, where i cooked on the pavement, coming near to death. in these moments, i was not thinking about dirt or worms. in these moments, i felt one with myself, whether worm or bug or bird.

then, i heard a loud fluttering overhead. a massive winged beast swung low, stretching its talons out in my direction. but, instead of impaling me, the claws sunk into the dirt and the bird perched in the ground directly in front of me. i gazed upon the beast with awe. she was the most vast creature i had ever laid eyes upon. her wings matched the stormy grey of the sky above, but her breast shared the blazing orange color of the rising sun.

she looked down upon me, silent and still.

"bird, devourer of worm! i surrender myself unto thee! may my flesh feed your young!" i called out.

she continued to stare.

"why do you hesitate? am i unworthy of nature's cycle?" i cried.

"your flesh serves me no purpose, as my eggs did not hatch this season. nature's cycle is clogged by my failures. it is not you who is unworthy," she chirped.

with these words, the bird shed her beastly form. we were now just two animals sat in the rain.

"then sustain yourself! it is my final purpose!" i pleaded.

"i shall not take from you that which i have already taken myself," she replied. "do you have no home, worm? i can take you there and you can reunite with loved ones."

as the rain poured upon me, i pictured home. the wormfolk i left behind lived on in ignorance as i ventured deeper into my own demise. what stories of me would they speak? a fool, a wormsenseless fool? just like all of the others? just like the stories i told of paul?

"okay, bird. if it must be. i'll tell them your story, the story of paul, the story of the bug. take me home," i said.

she plucked me from the earth, flapped her might wings, and shot over the land. we landed on the edge of the pavement.

"the rain grows too strong, worm. i cannot take you further. can you find your way home from here?" she asked.

"yes, bird, and i thank you," i replied.

we said our goodbyes and she darted away, over the horizon.

i now stand before the treacherous pavement, preparing my mind for the dangerous trek home. the next time i write, it will be from my familiar pile of dirt.
