the rain visited last night. a foolish and inevitable few wormfolk tried to cross the pavement and lost. normally i refrain from dwelling upon these losses. those wormfolk knew of their foolishness, and they knew of its consequences. this time, however, one of those fools was a dear friend of mine: paul wormtide.


he had awoken me before his departure, proclaiming, "chuck! rain falls, chuck!"

"as it does," i replied. "leave me alone. i have a worm quiz at worm school tomorrow, and i'd like to be well-rested for it!"

"no, chuck, you don't understand. i feel the pull, the urge to cross the pavement. i can't refuse this aching temptation!"

"where's your wormsense? it must already be waiting for you out there!"

"you feel it too, i know you do! it's plainly written in the motions of your worming and squirming. join me, chuck. if there's anything more to our sad wormlives, we can only find it out there, over the horizon!"

"i don't know what you're talking about."

paul shook his head and turned. "if that's true, chuck, then maybe i didn't really know you as well as i had thought," he said as he departed.


now i reflect on his final words to me. paul was correct in his assessment. i too, at times, feel a gust of soaring hope when i gaze upon the pavement. but i won't let such primal malfunctions inhibit my wormsense. worms, i say again, are pathetic creatures too feeble to sail along the powerful winds of hope. and as a worm, i must respect our nature as dictated by the worm gods. i will eat dirt, i will poop dirt. i will take worm quizzes covering the topic of dirt. such is worm life.
