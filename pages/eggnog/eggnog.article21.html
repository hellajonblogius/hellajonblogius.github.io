i had feared that i had been abandoned in the mud pit when i heard a rustle in the grass. from the depths of the grass forest emerged the familiar bulbous black bug. how relieving a feeling.


"mister worm! you're awake!" he called out, scattering over to my mud puddle.

"it seems so," i replied. "i owe you thanks, bug, for saving me."

"no thanks needed, mister worm."

"i insist, for i may need further assistance."

"well i'm sure i can help!"

"do you recall, bug, seeing another worm on the pavement? weeks ago? his name was paul."

"i see many worms out there, your kind are a curious folk!" the bug laughed. "i save the ones i can when the rain stops."

"but any named paul?" i implored. "this was weeks ago! do you have any recollection of a worm named paul?"

"hmm...paul...paul. i don't remember any pauls. what's he look like?"

"he resembled myself. pink, wormlike."

"sorry, mister worm, but i don't think i ever came across your friend." he concluded. "but i can take you to the worm reservoir. that's where i take all of the worms i save. maybe one of them has seen your friend."


this proposition sparked a dangerous feeling within me. it was the same feeling that brought me here, that propelled me and paul and many other worms out onto the pavement. it was the feeling that took a hold of my wormsense and strangled it into submission. it was hope, and it was dangerous. i hesitantly agreed.

the bug shifted his weight unnervingly. his shell split down the middle and shifted up above his head. the sun refracted off of it, dazing me in bands of green, blue, and purple. from out behind the shell sprouted two magnificent wings, shining like fragile crystals. he angled his titanic mandibles in my direction and began chopping his wings with increasing intensity. my wormheart galloped with vigor. he aimed his titanic mandibles in my direction, snatching me out of the mud and swiftly launched us into the air.

we passed over the grass forest with speeds unknown to wormkind. each blade shot under me faster than raindrops falling through the sky. my pathetic worm body flapped in the wind, completely disoriening me. but what an elating feeling. like i had suspected, paul was correct in his intuition: this concentration of sensation could only be found out here, over the horizon.

soon we landed in an empty pocket of dirt and the bug expressed his concern and confusion.


"where are all of the worms? there were six here, when i left!"

"perhaps they departed." i suggested.

"no, no, that can't be. it's dangerous in the grass forest, i told them. i told them it's dangerous, i told them to wait here."

"well if we cannot locate them it is to be presumed we cannot locate paul! we must search for them, immediately!"

"it's too dangerous, mister worm, i'm telling you." the bug insisted. "besides, if they're alive then they'll try to come back here. we should be here when they return."

the sun began to set.

"perhaps you're right. we shall wait out the night here."


and so, the night has crept over us. the bug has fallen asleep and i am stirring restlessly, awaiting dusk. while biding in the dirt may be the sensible plan, i find myself unable to rest in such wormly inaction. to think i have come so far only to cower in dirt, as if i had never left home. no, i think i will leave. i must find those worms and i will wait no longer.
