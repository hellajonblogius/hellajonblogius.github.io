♪Love Elegy - Mike Sunderland♪

hello. my name is chuck wormin, and i am a worm.

i don't like being a worm. worms are pathetic creatures. we wiggle and squirm around all day. we eat dirt and poop dirt. our sensory capabilities are meager at best. worms lack talent, drive, ambition. i am no different. i, too, am a worm. i prefer to worm around then conceive of a greater purpose. such is worm life.

i start my day by waking up and getting ready for worm school. i grab my worm pencil, my worm backpack, and i worm over to a pile of dirt some 10 feet from my own dirt pile. in the morning, we worm younglings practice worming through dirt. then we break for an hour of worm lunch, in which we eat dirt. after worm lunch, we are taught about rain. rain is a scary thing for wormfolk. when the moisture falls from above, we can move around better. many of us, whose worm ambition overtakes their wormsense, try to maneuver across the vast plane of pavement and across to the far dirt. the worm gods punish such ambition, ceasing the rain and leaving fools to dry out like raisins. sometimes i wonder if any worms make it to the far dirt. i wonder if there are more planes of pavement, more plots of far dirt. but then my wormsense kicks in and clears my mind of such thoughts. us worms have what we need and need what we have. there's no wormsense for pondering on the mysteries of extrawormial nature.

after worm school, i worm back to my dirt pile, where i worm around some more. for supper, i eat dirt. such is worm life.
