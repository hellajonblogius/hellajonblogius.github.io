i gazed out across the pavement. the rain fell hard. as i thought of returning home, i felt hope.

i ventured out onto the pavement, seizing against the cold water that coated it. but my mind stayed ready and my heart stayed steady. with all of the might in my wormish composition, i wormed inch by inch, hour by hour, along the slick pavement. through the thunderous clouds, i saw the sun sink below the horizon, rendering me sightless. but i continued onward in a single direction, guided by wormsense. throughout the night, i waged a fierce battle against the rain, the pavement, and my own exhaustion. finally, as the morning sun rose into the sky, i reached the stretch of dirt i call home.

i was greeted by my wormkin with awe and surprise, with tears of joy and relief. i was rushed to my dirt pile to rest, as i had spent a lifetime of energy in less than a week.

despite pure exhaustion, i cannot sleep. instead, i write from my dirt pile. and though my routine returns with me, eating dirt, pooping dirt, i have come home with something new. stories of paul and of bugs and of birds. stories untold of worm courage and worm greatness, stories that shed light onto our true nature as worms.

we worms hold within our wormish vessel more than previously thought possible. we are worms: capable, brave, resilient! no longer do we choose between either foolishness or cowardice. we are worms! when the rain next falls upon the pavement, when our hearts are next filled with great gusts of hope, we will not hesitate. we will live in search of something that can never be found in the comfort of routine. we will live and die by it! such is worm life!
