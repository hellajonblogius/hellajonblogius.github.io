##### Section 1: AMAZON #####

Let's start with Amazon returns. Below are four types of Amazon returns at UPS. The type of return that Amazon offers a customer is based on the size of the time, the location of the return, and the preference of the seller.

1. No box, no label
No box, no label returns give customers a QR code that says "The UPS Store" and "No box, no label" on it. These are 100% free returns. As suggested by the name, the customer doesn't need to package the item and they don't need to provide a shipping label. When we scan the QR code, it prints out a special label that is NOT a shipping label. We place the items in a clear plastic bag, tape it using the label, then consolidate multiple of these returns into one big box.

2. Customer packed
These returns give customers a QR code that says "The UPS Store" and "Customer packed" on it. Unsurprisingly, these items need to be packed by the customer, including tape. When we scan the QR code, it prints out a UPS shipping label. If customers bring in a customer packed return without packaging, they will be charged for a box. If the box is large and needs tape, the customer will be charged for tape.
****IMPORTANT NOTE****
Items cannot be returned in PRODUCT BOXES unless there is already an Amazon shipping label on it. For example, if customers are returning a PS5, the prodcut box is the box that is made by Sony and has all of the promotional shit on it. Amazon isn't supposed to ship product boxes without packaging (aka putting it in an Amazon box/bag), but if they do, we are allowed to put a UPS label on top of the Amazon label.

This rules causes me so much fucking grief. People always always always think that they can use the product box. Section 8: Conclusion

I used to be pretty optimistic when it comes to people. I never really liked talking to them, but I believed that most people are just normal and kinda are like me. This job has changed me. I am no longer so sure. Even the good customers, the ones who are in and out in 30 seconds, the ones who have the slightest clue, even those people radiate something. Something that makes me feel bad. I used to feel that other people have the same sense of interiority that I do. Even if I couldn't relate to their perspective, I could imagine that they had some sort of soul. I don't feel that way anymore. Maybe it's a reflection on the changes that I've seen in myself. Perhaps the UPS customers take on the form of my own anger, my own entitlement, my own stupidity and self-importance. Am I no longer normal, no longer like myself? Is my mind duller than it was, my soul weaker?

Sometimes a customer will approach the counter and smile, make a joke or tell a light-hearted story. They'll place their package next to the scale instead of onto it and I'll feel my blood pressure spike. I'll think some mean thoughts like all of the ones I wrote above. I'll think, "Fuck you, stupid jackass. Why the fuck would you put it on the counter and not on the scale? What do you think the scale's there for, decoration?" And usually that'll be it and I'll move onto the next customer. But sometimes, they'll be the last customer in line, and when they leave I'll be alone, I'll have time to think. I'll think, "Who's actually at fault? Is it the person who put the box on the counter or is it the person who has spent all day keeping track? Which person's responsible for my misery?"
