my life as a teenage worm part 6: the worm gods' dreaded recompence -- June 18, 2026

in the night, i sneakily wormed out of the safety of the worm reservoir. while it disconcerted me to abandon such a helpful companion, i knew the bug would never allow such impulsion to dictate our journey. and while i can acknowledge the flaws in my plan to search for the rogue worms alone, i maintain that immediate action was necessary.

i ventured into the grass forest, scouring for any indication of worm life. after some time, i came across a horrific sight: a worm, sliced cleanly down the middle. i approached carefully.


"fellow worm? can you here me?" i asked.

"who's there? i cannot see. i'm afraid i'm dying" he replied.

"my name is chuck wormin. i am a worm."

"yes of course. it was a bird. it dove from the heavens, snatched my backside."

"where are there others? from the worm reseviore?"

"they scattered after the attack, abandoned me. i cannot blame them. us worms are born cowards."

"was one of them named paul? paul wormtide?"

"paul wormtide? no, no. paul is long dead. he's on the pavement, shriveled and useless. he died as he lived, as we all die and live: cowering."


this stunning revelation is a moment i shall never forget. each segment of my worm body shivered as my blood turned to ice. i must have let my grief escape onto my face, as he followed up:


"oh you can't be surprised. he was a fool, you are a fool! all of us out here are fools. you know this."


recounting the dying halfworm's venomous words fills me for the same disgust and hatred that i had felt then.


"paul was no fool! he was an adventurer!" i exploded.

"listen to yourself, you are lost!" he accused. "go home, worm, or you will soon end up like myself!"

"i'd choose death over being a worm! a vile, ugly, lonely death!" i proclaimed.


the dying worm coughed and began fading. his body clung to life, but his soul longed for death. his final breath escaped him, saying:


"there is no such choice to be made. such is worm life." and he died.


his parting words hit me like the hot sun. devastated, i wormed underground and slept. it is now morning and, for the first time since leaving home, i feel truly lost. i hardly even have the drive to return to the worm reservoir. maybe i should wait for the bug to come find me, or for that matter, a bird.
