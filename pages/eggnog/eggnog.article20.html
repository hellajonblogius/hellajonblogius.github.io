whether in defiance or by the blessing of the worm gods, i am not yet dead.

last night, i embraced my erratic temptations and trod pavement in search of something. the ground was so solid and the rain was so cold. they demanded a constitution of which i did not hold. worms are not built for such harsh sensations. i hardly had space to breathe amongst all the fear that engulfed my feeble worm brain. my frantic worming and squirming exerted me to the point of exhaustion, and i promptly lost consciousness. luckily, the rain fell throughout the night and into the morning. my foolishness, too, survived the night. upon awaking, i felt no urge to return home. the morning sun soothed my racing mind, and i continued onward.

after some time, with endless pavement stretching over the horizon, my greatest fear manifested. the rain dried up and the hot sun of noon shone overhead. the heat choked out any moisture on the ground and in my body. the rough pavement gripped my skin like imps dragging me to worm hell, where the putrid hot sun cooked me alive. i was sharing the fate of many foolish wormfolk before me, yet i felt no regret.

however, as my vision faded and my soul prepared its departure, i heard a rugged voice.

"hang on there, mister worm."

a large bug stood over my sizzling body with a concerned visage. sunlight bounced off of his plated shell with a jarring multicolored refraction. his head sported thick mandibles and short antennae. my exhaustion proceeded my curiosity and i once again fell out of consciousness. i awoke in a shallow, muddy puddle shaded by towering blades of surrounding grass. looking around, i could see no bug.

it is in this puddle where i currently remain, awaiting the bug's return.
